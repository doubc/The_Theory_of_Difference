## Theoretical Background

### Johnson Graph $J(4,2)$

The Johnson graph $J(N,k)$ has vertices corresponding to $k$-element subsets of an $N$-element set, with edges connecting subsets that differ by exactly one element.

For $N=4, k=2$:
- **Vertices**: $\binom{4}{2} = 6$ vertices (all 2-element subsets of {1,2,3,4})
- **Edges**: Each vertex connects to $2(4-2) = 4$ others
- **Total edges**: $6 \times 4 / 2 = 12$ edges
- **Geometric realization**: Regular octahedron

### A1' Constraint (Transverse Emergence)

Axiom A1' requires lateral structure with U(1) phase coherence. For $N=4$, this selects the "midsection" of the hypercube ${0,1}^4$, which corresponds to states with exactly 2 bits set to 1.

### Simplicial Complex Construction

From the Johnson graph, we construct a 2-dimensional simplicial complex:
- **0-simplices (vertices)**: 6 vertices of $J(4,2)$
- **1-simplices (edges)**: 12 edges of $J(4,2)$
- **2-simplices (faces)**: 8 triangular faces (corresponding to 3-cycles in the graph)

### Homology Theory

The homology groups are computed via the chain complex:

$$C_2 \xrightarrow{\partial_2} C_1 \xrightarrow{\partial_1} C_0$$

where:
- $C_k$ is the free abelian group generated by $k$-simplices
- $\partial_k$ are boundary operators satisfying $\partial_{k-1} \circ \partial_k = 0$

Betti numbers are calculated as:
$$b_k = \dim \ker \partial_k - \dim \operatorname{im} \partial_{k+1}$$

Using rank-nullity theorem:
$$b_k = f_k - \operatorname{rank}(\partial_k) - \operatorname{rank}(\partial_{k+1})$$

where $f_k$ is the number of $k$-simplices.

## Methodology

### Step 1: Vertex Enumeration

Vertices of $J(4,2)$ are labeled by 2-element subsets:
```
v0 = {1,2}, v1 = {1,3}, v2 = {1,4}, v3 = {2,3}, v4 = {2,4}, v5 = {3,4}
```

### Step 2: Edge Construction

Edges connect vertices differing by one element. Total: 12 edges.

Example:
- e0: v0↔v1 ({1,2} ↔ {1,3}, difference: 2→3)
- e1: v0↔v2 ({1,2} ↔ {1,4}, difference: 2→4)
- ...

### Step 3: Face Identification

Faces correspond to 3-cycles in the graph. For $J(4,2)$, there are 8 triangular faces.

Example:
- f0: v0→v1→v3→v0 (cycle: {1,2}→{1,3}→{2,3}→{1,2})
- f1: v0→v2→v4→v0 (cycle: {1,2}→{1,4}→{2,4}→{1,2})
- ...

### Step 4: Boundary Matrix Construction

**$\partial_1$ matrix (6×12)**: Maps edges to vertices
- Entry $\partial_1[i,j] = +1$ if edge $j$ ends at vertex $i$
- Entry $\partial_1[i,j] = -1$ if edge $j$ starts at vertex $i$
- Entry $\partial_1[i,j] = 0$ otherwise

**$\partial_2$ matrix (12×8)**: Maps faces to edges
- Entry $\partial_2[j,k] = +1$ if edge $j$ is in face $k$ with consistent orientation
- Entry $\partial_2[j,k] = -1$ if edge $j$ is in face $k$ with opposite orientation
- Entry $\partial_2[j,k] = 0$ otherwise

### Betti Numbers

$$b_0 = f_0 - \operatorname{rank}(\partial_1) = 6 - 5 = 1$$
