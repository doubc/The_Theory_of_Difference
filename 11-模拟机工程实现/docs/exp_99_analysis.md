### The Real Pattern: CIV Explosion Is Seed-Dependent, Not Feedback-Dependent

The fact that removing CIV from turning point detection didn't reduce overall CIV means
the NSE-CIV feedback loop was NOT the primary driver of CIV explosion.

Instead, the CIV explosion appears to be driven by **structural conditions that vary by seed**:

1. **Seeds 42/142**: CIV exploded to 189/178 without any NSE-CIV feedback. This means
   the narrative recursion operator itself is generating CIV events at a high rate for
   these specific random seeds.

2. **Seeds 542/642**: CIV was tamed from 184/182 → 14/13. For these seeds, the NSE-CIV
   feedback WAS a significant amplifier, and removing it helped.

3. **Seeds 242/342/442/742**: CIV stayed reasonable (6-12) regardless.

### New Hypothesis: CIV Explosion Is Driven by Narrative Recursion, Not NSE

The CIV count is accumulated from `narrative_level_dist.get('CIVILIZATION', 0)` across
step results. This means CIV events are generated by the **narrative recursion operator**,
not by the NSE turning point detector.

The NSE-CIV feedback loop (exp_98 finding) was a real phenomenon but only amplified
the explosion in seeds 542/642. The primary driver is the narrative recursion operator
generating CIVILIZATION-level events at a rate that varies by seed.

### Why Did Seeds 42/142 Get Worse?

In exp_98, seeds 42/142 had CIV=15/9 (reasonable). In exp_99, they have CIV=189/178.
This is counterintuitive — removing a feedback loop should not increase CIV.

Possible explanation: **The CIV weight in turning point detection was actually acting as
a brake on the narrative recursion operator.** When CIV contributed to turning point
detection, it created history, which increased NSI, which may have stabilized the
narrative recursion. Without CIV in the turning point detector, this stabilizing
mechanism is gone.

This suggests the relationship between NSE and narrative recursion is more complex
than a simple feedback loop.

### n_steps Anomaly

All seeds show n_steps=160-240 instead of the expected 160 (1600/10). This means
the system is converging/sealing much earlier than in exp_95 (which had n_steps=160).
The `active_count=0` confirms the system becomes inactive early.

This early convergence may be related to the ILP's strong protection (ilp_floor=20.0
for all seeds), which prevents INSTITUTIONAL consumption and may cause premature
stabilization.

## Root Cause Reassessment

The exp_98 diagnosis was partially wrong. The NSE-CIV feedback loop is real but
secondary. The primary driver of CIV explosion appears to be:

1. **Narrative recursion operator generates CIV events at a seed-dependent rate**
2. **ILP strong protection (floor=20) causes premature convergence** (n_steps=160-240)
3. **Early convergence means fewer steps for CIV to accumulate, but the rate per step
   is higher for certain seeds**

The CIV explosion in seeds 42/142 (CIV=189/178) with n_steps=240 means the CIV
accumulation rate is ~0.78 per step — extremely high. This suggests the narrative
recursion operator is generating CIVILIZATION-level events almost every step for
these seeds.

## Recommendations

### Immediate: Investigate Narrative Recursion CIV Generation

The narrative recursion operator's CIVILIZATION event generation rate needs to be
examined. Why do seeds 42/142 generate CIV at ~0.78/step while seeds 242/342
generate it at ~0.025/step?

### Short-Term: Relax ILP Floor

The ILP floor=20.0 for all seeds may be causing premature convergence. If the
system converges too early, the narrative recursion doesn't have time to stabilize
naturally. Consider reducing the floor or making it adaptive.

### Medium-Term: Redesign CIV Accumulation

CIV should not be a simple count of CIVILIZATION-level narrative events. It should
reflect structural complexity, not just event frequency. Consider:
- CIV as a measure of INSTITUTIONAL-to-CIVILIZATION conversion efficiency
- CIV as a function of cross-scale coupling strength (CSCI)
- CIV capped by GBC coherence

### Long-Term: Separate Narrative Recursion from CIV Measurement

The current design conflates narrative activity (which should be free to vary) with
structural complexity measurement (which should be constrained). These should be
separate concerns.

## Conclusion

exp_99 shows that removing CIV from turning point detection does not fix H5. The CIV
explosion is driven by the narrative recursion operator's CIVILIZATION event generation,
not by the NSE-CIV feedback loop. The fix needs to be at the narrative recursion level,
not at the NSE level.
